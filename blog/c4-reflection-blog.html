EDA Reflection

30 November 2015

What's your take on the DBC / EDA experience?

What Shereef is talking about is exactly how I am treating this course. Some people ask me "Oh, so do you get a qualification or a certificate in something?" and I say "I don't actually know, but it is irrelevant. This course is about learning and giving my all and coming learning skills. My outcome is the skills I learnt, not a qualification.". To me this means I am giving this course my all to soak up and to learn as much as possible, but also make friends and have fun!

What are your impressions?

My impressions so far are really good. I love coming to the EDA offices each day and leave with a smile on my face. People here are more than happy to help when you are confused and chat when you are in the kitchen. I am loving my experience.

How do you see yourself engaging with this type of culture?

By being present, helping when I have knowledge that others don't and giving it my all in everything I do.

Have your expectations of EDA changed? If so, how?

No, I came into EDA with high expectionation and a ready to go attitude and that hasn't changed. If anything, I am even more energised to work hard and make this an amazing experience for myself and my pairs.

Are you excited to participate in this kind of learning environment? Does it make you nervous?

This is the kind of enviroment I excell in, so I am really excited. It does not make me nervous, it makes me excited.
